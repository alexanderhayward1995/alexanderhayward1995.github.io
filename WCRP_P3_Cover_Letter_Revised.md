# Cover Letter — WCRP Science and Communications Officer (P.3)

## Revised Version

---

Dear WMO Selection Panel,

From deploying ice-temperature buoys in Qaanaaq, Greenland, to coordinating multinational satellite climate data records and Arctic assessment chapters, my career has been built around connecting cryosphere observations to actionable climate knowledge. I am writing to apply for the position of Science and Communications Officer (P.3) with the World Climate Research Programme, where I believe I can contribute meaningfully to the coordination of WCRP core projects and Lighthouse Activities, and to strengthening WCRP's scientific communications and visibility.

**Programme Coordination and Scientific Support**

My current work maps closely onto the programmatic coordination this role requires. As Lead Author for the AMAP 2026 assessment on Arctic and Subarctic marine heatwaves, I coordinate a multidisciplinary team of 15+ scientists across seven countries—spanning physical oceanography, ecology, and social science—synthesising diverse inputs into a coherent, policy-relevant chapter for Arctic Council stakeholders. This mirrors the cross-cutting coordination needed to support CliC, Safe Landing Climates, and My Climate Risk.

As Principal Investigator of the RetroKlim project, I manage the full programme lifecycle from concept through delivery, including budget oversight and reporting to national funding agencies. Across my ESA and Copernicus work package leadership roles (PHYTO-CCI, FOCCUS, ObsSea4Clim), I align scientific activities across multinational partners and ensure delivery of GCOS-compliant climate data records. In total, I have secured over €500,000 in competitive funding and contributed to international consortia exceeding €2.5 million—experience directly relevant to supporting WCRP's fundraising and budget coordination activities.

I am familiar with the WCRP organisational landscape, including the roles of its co-sponsors (WMO, ISC, IOC-UNESCO), the structure of its Core Projects and Lighthouse Activities, and the WCRP Science and Implementation Plan. My existing work with ESA CCI programmes and international assessments means I understand the coordination challenges of aligning diverse scientific communities around shared deliverables—the core function of this role.

**Scientific Communication and Outreach**

I bring experience across the full spectrum of science communication, from peer-reviewed publication (including a first-author paper in *Nature Climate Change*) to policy briefings for Greenlandic ministers and contributions to the Copernicus European State of the Climate Report 2026. Through the AMAP assessment, I routinely distil complex, multidisciplinary research into clear narratives tailored to non-specialist audiences.

Beyond traditional outputs, I have practical experience with the operational side of science communication that this role emphasises. I developed the PhytoClass GUI to make complex datasets accessible to a broad user community, and I am experienced in producing content across formats—technical reports, conference presentations, media engagement, and digital outreach. I would welcome the opportunity to apply these skills to developing WCRP's e-newsletter, web presence, social media strategy, and fundraising communications, ensuring consistent branding and messaging across the programme.

**International Experience and Inclusive Collaboration**

My career spans the UK, Canada, New Zealand, and Denmark, with polar fieldwork in Greenland, the central Arctic, and Antarctica. I have collaborated with partners across Europe, Asia, and the Southern Hemisphere, including KOPRI, the Australian Antarctic Division, and institutions across North America. I have also built partnerships with Māori researchers in Aotearoa New Zealand and the Greenland Institute of Natural Resources, embedding locally grounded approaches in international monitoring programmes. These experiences have prepared me to work effectively within WCRP's global, multicultural research networks and to engage with Early to Mid-Career Researchers through the WCRP Academy.

I bring pace, resilience, and a collaborative working style. I am committed to delivering science that serves society, and I would be glad to contribute to WCRP's coordination and communication mission as the programme advances its Implementation Plan and supports the climate research community through a critical period.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
Alexander Hayward
